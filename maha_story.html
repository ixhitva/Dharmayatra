Historical Context

The Mahabharata, one of India's two major Sanskrit epics alongside the Ramayana, is traditionally attributed to the sage Vyasa. Composed between the 3rd century BCE and the 3rd century CE, its oral traditions likely date back earlier. Set in the ancient kingdom of Bharatavarsha, the epic narrates the dynastic conflict between the Pandava and Kaurava cousins, leading to the monumental Kurukshetra War. This narrative blends historical events with mythological elements, offering insights into ancient Indian society, governance, and warfare.

Influence and Legacy

The Mahabharata's impact extends beyond India, influencing Southeast Asian cultures through adaptations in dance, theater, and literature. Its stories have been reimagined in modern media, from television serials to novels, keeping its relevance alive. The epic's teachings on dharma and governance continue to inspire ethical discussions, making it a cornerstone of global literary and philosophical heritage as of July 8, 2025.
