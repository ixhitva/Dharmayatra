Ramayana Timeline

Circa 12300 BCE
Birth of Dasharatha
King Dasharatha, future ruler of Ayodhya, is born into the Ikshvaku dynasty. His reign will be marked by his commitment to dharma and the challenges of fulfilling his royal duties, setting the stage for the Ramayana’s central events.
Dec 12241 – Jan 12241 BCE
Dasharatha’s Putra-Kameshthi Yajna
Desiring heirs, King Dasharatha performs the Putra-Kameshthi Yajna under the guidance of Sage Rishyashringa. The ritual yields divine nectar, which Dasharatha distributes among his queens, leading to the births of Rama, Lakshmana, Bharata, and Shatrughna.
29 Nov 12240 BCE
Rama Janma (Birth of Rama)
Rama, the seventh avatar of Vishnu, is born to King Dasharatha and Queen Kausalya in Ayodhya. His birth fulfills divine prophecies, marking the arrival of the hero destined to uphold dharma and defeat Ravana.
4 Dec 12224 BCE
Vishwamitra Arrives in Ayodhya
Sage Vishwamitra visits Ayodhya to request Rama’s assistance in protecting his yajna from demons. Dasharatha reluctantly agrees, and Rama, accompanied by Lakshmana, embarks on his first heroic journey.
7 Dec 12224 BCE
Rama and Lakshmana Kill Tataka
Rama, guided by Vishwamitra, slays the demoness Tataka, who was terrorizing the forest hermits. This act marks Rama’s first triumph over evil and establishes his prowess as a warrior.
8 Dec 12224 BCE
Arrival at Siddhashrama
Rama and Lakshmana arrive at Vishwamitra’s Siddhashrama, a sacred hermitage, to prepare for protecting the sage’s yajna from demonic disruptions.
9–14 Dec 12224 BCE
Protecting Vishwamitra’s Yajna
Rama and Lakshmana vigilantly guard Vishwamitra’s yajna, defeating demons Maricha and Subahu who attempt to disrupt the sacred ritual, showcasing their martial skills.
15 Dec 12224 BCE
Journey to Mithila
Rama and Lakshmana, accompanied by Vishwamitra, set out for Mithila, engaging in late-night conversations with the sage, who imparts wisdom and narrates ancient tales.
17 Dec 12224 BCE
Arrival at Vishala
Rama, Lakshmana, and Vishwamitra reach the city of Vishala en route to Mithila, resting and preparing for the significant events awaiting them in Janaka’s kingdom.
18 Dec 12224 BCE
Rama Meets Ahalya
At Ahalya’s ashram, Rama liberates her from a curse by touching her stone form with his foot, restoring her to life and reuniting her with Sage Gautama.
18 Dec 12224 BCE
Arrival in Mithila
Rama, Lakshmana, and Vishwamitra arrive in Mithila, the capital of King Janaka’s kingdom, where Rama is destined to participate in Sita’s swayamvara.
19 Dec 12224 BCE
Rama Lifts Shiva-Dhanushya
At Sita’s swayamvara, Rama lifts and breaks the divine Shiva-Dhanushya, a feat no other suitor could achieve, winning Sita’s hand in marriage.
29 Dec 12224 BCE
Dasharatha Arrives in Mithila
King Dasharatha arrives in Mithila to attend the wedding preparations for Rama and Sita, strengthening the alliance between Ayodhya and Mithila.
30 Dec 12224 BCE
Janaka Completes Yajna
King Janaka performs a grand yajna to consecrate the upcoming marriage of Sita to Rama, fulfilling his duties as a father and ruler.
1–2 Jan 12223 BCE
Rama-Sita Wedding Decided
The date for Rama and Sita’s wedding is finalized, along with the marriages of Lakshmana, Bharata, and Shatrughna to Urmila, Mandavi, and Shrutakirti, respectively.
4–5 Jan 12223 BCE
Rama-Sita Wedding
Rama marries Sita in a grand ceremony in Mithila, joined by the weddings of his brothers. This union binds the divine couple, setting the stage for their shared journey.
20–21 Dec 12223 BCE
Rama’s Coronation Scheduled
King Dasharatha announces Rama as his successor, scheduling his coronation as the crown prince of Ayodhya, sparking joy among the people but envy in Queen Kaikeyi’s heart.
20–21 Dec 12223 BCE
Rama’s Vanavas (Exile)
Influenced by Manthara, Queen Kaikeyi demands Rama’s exile for 14 years and Bharata’s coronation. Rama, honoring his father’s word, leaves Ayodhya with Sita and Lakshmana.
25–26 Dec 12223 BCE
Death of Dasharatha
Heartbroken by Rama’s exile, King Dasharatha succumbs to grief and dies, leaving Ayodhya in mourning and without a king.
27–29 Dec 12223 BCE
Messenger Travels to Kekaya
Messengers are dispatched to Kekaya to summon Bharata, who is unaware of his father’s death and Rama’s exile, to return to Ayodhya.
30 Dec 12223 – 5 Jan 12222 BCE
Bharata’s Journey to Ayodhya
Bharata, informed of his father’s death and Rama’s exile, journeys from Kekaya to Ayodhya, distressed by the events orchestrated by his mother, Kaikeyi.
6–7 Jan 12222 BCE
Bharata Performs Dasharatha’s Rituals
Bharata performs the 11th and 12th day funeral rites for King Dasharatha, fulfilling his filial duties amidst the turmoil in Ayodhya.
9 Jan 12222 BCE
Vasistha Requests Bharata to Accept Throne
Sage Vasistha urges Bharata to accept the throne of Ayodhya, but Bharata refuses, insisting that Rama is the rightful heir.
26 Jan 12222 BCE
Bharata Leaves Ayodhya to Meet Rama
Determined to bring Rama back, Bharata leaves Ayodhya to meet his exiled brother at Chitrakuta, hoping to persuade him to return as king.
10 Apr 12222 BCE
Bharata Meets Rama at Chitrakuta
Bharata meets Rama at Chitrakuta, pleading with him to return to Ayodhya. Rama refuses, upholding his father’s word, demonstrating his commitment to dharma.
11 Apr 12222 BCE
Bharata Asks for Rama’s Sandals
Unable to persuade Rama, Bharata requests his sandals to place on the throne of Ayodhya, symbolizing Rama’s rule while Bharata governs as his regent.
28 Mar 12210 BCE
Rama and Khara Fight
In the Dandaka forest, Rama battles and defeats the demon Khara and his army, protecting the sages and escalating tensions with Ravana.
May–Jul 12210 BCE
Ravana Abducts Sita
Ravana, enraged by Rama’s actions, abducts Sita from Panchavati using a deception involving a golden deer, setting the stage for the epic conflict.
Oct–Nov 12210 BCE
Ravana Sets Deadline for Sita
Ravana gives Sita a deadline to yield to his desires, imprisoning her in the Ashoka grove in Lanka, where she remains steadfast in her devotion to Rama.
21 Sep 12210 BCE
Vali-Sugriva Fight (1st Round)
Rama allies with Sugriva, who challenges his brother Vali to a fight. Rama observes but does not intervene in this first round, assessing the situation.
22 Sep 12210 BCE
Vali-Sugriva Fight (2nd Round)
In the second fight, Rama aids Sugriva by shooting Vali with an arrow, killing him and restoring Sugriva to the throne of Kishkindha.
22 Sep 12210 – 13 Jan 12209 BCE
Rama-Lakshmana in Kishkindha
Rama and Lakshmana reside outside Kishkindha, waiting for Sugriva to organize the vanara army to search for Sita.
After 13 Jan 12209 BCE
Vanara Search Parties Depart
Sugriva dispatches vanara search parties across the world to locate Sita, with Hanuman leading the southern group towards Lanka.
Mid-Jul 12209 BCE
Angada’s Frustration
Angada, leading a search party, grows frustrated with the lack of progress in finding Sita, facing challenges in the southern regions.
27–30 Aug 12209 BCE
Hanuman in Lanka
Hanuman leaps across the ocean to Lanka, locates Sita in the Ashoka grove, and delivers Rama’s message, boosting her hope.
30 Aug 12209 BCE
Hanuman Returns from Lanka
Hanuman flies back from Lanka, reporting Sita’s location to Rama and Sugriva, galvanizing the vanara army for the rescue mission.
29 Sep 12209 BCE
Rama and Vanara Army March to Lanka
Rama, Lakshmana, and the vanara army, led by Sugriva, begin their march from Kishkindha towards Lanka to confront Ravana.
8–18 Dec 12209 BCE
Construction of Nala-Setu
Under Nala’s supervision, the vanara army constructs the Nala-Setu (Rama’s bridge) across the ocean to reach Lanka, a remarkable engineering feat.
22 Dec 12209 BCE
Rama and Army on Suvela
Rama and the vanara army encamp on Mount Suvela, overlooking Lanka, preparing for the impending war against Ravana’s forces.
24 Dec 12209 – 7 Jan 12208 BCE
The Lanka War
Rama leads the vanara army in a fierce war against Ravana’s forces. After intense battles, Rama slays Ravana, rescuing Sita and restoring dharma.
12 Jan 12208 BCE
Rama at Bharadwaj’s Ashram
On his return to Ayodhya, Rama visits Sage Bharadwaj’s ashram, receiving blessings and preparing for his homecoming.
13 Jan 12208 BCE
Rama Meets Bharata
Rama reunites with Bharata in Ayodhya, who joyfully relinquishes the throne, having ruled as Rama’s regent during his exile.
13 Jan 12208 BCE
Rama’s Coronation
Rama crowned the rightful king of Ayodhya. The city joyously celebrates.
