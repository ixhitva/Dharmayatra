Historical Context

The Ramayana, one of India's two major Sanskrit epics alongside the Mahabharata, is traditionally attributed to the sage Valmiki. Composed between the 7th and 4th centuries BCE, with oral traditions likely predating this period, the epic narrates the life of Lord Rama, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, who embodies dharma (duty) and righteousness. Set in the ancient kingdoms of Kosala and Lanka, the Ramayana chronicles Rama‚Äôs journey from prince to exile, his battle against the demon king Ravana, and his return as king of Ayodhya. The epic blends historical settings with mythological elements, offering insights into ancient Indian society, governance, and values.

Cultural Significance

The Ramayana is a cornerstone of Indian culture, shaping moral and ethical frameworks through its teachings on dharma, devotion, and sacrifice.
